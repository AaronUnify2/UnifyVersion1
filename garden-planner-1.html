Garden beds. Each rectangle is a bed drawn to scale (grid squares are 30 cm / ~1 ft). Drag a bed to move it and pull the blue corner to resize. Press ✎ on a bed to name it, set exact dimensions and add plantings — the app checks how many plants fit from their spacing and estimates the harvest. Switch to List for the same beds as a table.

Greenhouse. This is your seed-starting nursery. Add a tray for anything you start under cover; the app suggests the sowing date (worked back from your last frost), predicts germination, and tells you the week the seedlings should be ready to move outside. Point a tray at a destination bed so the calendar connects sowing → transplanting.

Calendar. Everything here is computed — nothing is entered by hand. The season chart shows each planting's phases (amber = started under cover, green = growing outside, purple = harvest window) against your frost dates (dashed red lines). Below it, the week-by-week task list tells you exactly what to do and when, and the harvest outlook totals what you can expect to pick.

Climate settings

Frost dates are the anchor for every calculation. Use your local average last spring frost and first autumn frost (month and day — the year shown is just your planning year).
Picking a zone fills approximate average frost dates below — close enough for most gardens, and every field stays editable. Southern hemisphere: presets use northern-style dates, so pick the closest zone and shift the dates by ~6 months, or enter your own.

AI instruction files

Give one of these files to an AI assistant in a chat, together with your raw information ("my last frost is around May 1st", a seed-packet photo, a variety list…). The file tells the model exactly what JSON to produce. Save the model's output as a .json file and bring it back here with Import.

Climate spec → climate.json

Converts frost dates, hardiness zone and location notes into this app's climate format.

Plant spec → plants.json

Converts seed packets, catalogue pages or variety descriptions into plant records (timing, spacing, yield). Imported plants merge into your library.
