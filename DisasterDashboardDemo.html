ℹ️ Weather Context — Southern Downs

The Southern Downs experiences two distinct weather hazards. Thunderstorms and hail can be very localised — a severe storm may affect one town while nearby areas remain dry. Sustained rainfall across the Granite Belt and ranges causes regional flooding in the Condamine and associated catchments. These are typically separate events. For flooding information see the Flood tab.

ℹ️ Flood Context — Southern Downs

Flooding in the Southern Downs is typically driven by persistent regional rainfall across the Granite Belt and Main Range. The Condamine River and its tributaries can rise rapidly — the 14 crossings on Condamine River Road in particular require a 4WD and should be avoided if rain is forecast or the electric sign at the base indicates closure. Check the Disaster Dashboard map for real-time crossing status and flood camera images.
