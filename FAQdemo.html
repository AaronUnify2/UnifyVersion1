  • My neighbour's dog barks constantly. Can Council do something?
    ⌄

    Yes. Persistent barking is a noise nuisance Council can investigate under local laws. The process works in steps:

    1. Try direct contact first. In many cases the owner isn't aware — a friendly note often resolves it without Council involvement.
    2. Lodge a service request via Council's online service request form or by calling 1300 697 372.
    3. Keep a noise diary. Council will ask you to record dates, times, and how the barking affects you. This is essential — without it, Council has limited ability to take action.
    4. Council investigates. Once the diary is received, a Local Laws Officer will contact the dog owner.
    Download the Barking Dogs Fact Sheet and Noise Diary to get started.
  • Why does Council need a noise diary? Why can't an officer just come and listen?
    ⌄

    A noise diary creates an objective record showing a pattern — which Local Laws Officers need to demonstrate a genuine nuisance under the legislation. It also protects you by documenting the problem factually.

  • How do I register my dog?
    ⌄

    Download the Dog Registration Application Form and return it to Council. If applicable, also email proof of desexing or a pension card to mail@sdrc.qld.gov.au.

    Full details: Dog Registration — SDRC website.

  • How many animals am I allowed to keep on my property?
    ⌄

    Permitted numbers vary by property type and zone under local bylaws and the planning scheme. The Keeping of Animals Fact Sheet sets out permitted numbers by animal type.

    To keep more animals than permitted, a Keeping of Animals permit is required. Call 1300 697 372 to discuss your situation before applying.

  • My dog is missing or has been impounded. What do I do?
    ⌄

    Call Council on 1300 697 372 as soon as possible with your dog's breed, colour, size, and tag number. If impounded, a Local Laws Officer will advise the collection time, release fee, and whether registration is required before release.

    You can also check the Impounded and Rescued Animals page.

  • I've found a stray dog. What should I do?
    ⌄

    Call Council on 1300 697 372. A Local Laws Officer will attend to collect the animal. Please don't attempt to physically restrain an unknown dog — even a friendly animal can bite when stressed.

  • I've been attacked by a dog. What do I do?
    ⌄
    If anyone needs emergency medical attention, call 000 first.

    Once you're safe, call Council on 1300 697 372. A Local Laws Officer will attend the scene. You will also need to submit a Statement of Dog Attack form — email the completed form to mail@sdrc.qld.gov.au. When you call, have ready: the location, a description of the dog, whether it is still in the area, and the time of the attack.

  • I've been told I have a water leak. How do I find it?
    ⌄

    Many leaks can be found without a plumber. Check inside first — a running toilet is the most common cause. Add a few drops of food colouring to the cistern and wait 10 minutes without flushing; if colour appears in the bowl, the cistern is leaking. Also check all taps, showerheads, and washing machine connections.

    Outside, look for unusually green or boggy ground — this can indicate an underground pipe failure even when there's no visible surface water. Check garden timers and hose guns too.

    The Checking for Water Leaks Fact Sheet includes a method for testing underground pipes without a plumber.
  • There's a leak at the meter or on the footpath — is that Council's responsibility?
    ⌄

    Council's side (the meter itself, or the pipe between the water main and the meter): report to Council immediately on 1300 697 372.

    Your side (the pipe running from the meter into the property): this is the property owner's responsibility. Consider shutting off the water at the meter while you arrange a licensed plumber.

    Not sure which side? Call 1300 697 372 and we can help work it out.

  • Can I get a rates adjustment if I had an unexpected leak?
    ⌄

    In some circumstances Council may consider an adjustment for water lost to an undetected leak. Contact Council's Water Team on 1300 697 372 to discuss. Having your repair invoice available will assist with the assessment.

  • How can I monitor my water usage between bills?
    ⌄

    The My SDRC Water portal connects to your Smart Water Meter and lets you view near-real-time usage and set a custom alert threshold to catch a leak early. Sign-up details are on page 4 of your rates notice. See also: Smart Water Meters.

  • Where is my water meter?
    ⌄

    Water meters are usually near the front boundary of the property close to the street — often in a small covered box set into the ground. If you can't find yours, call 1300 697 372 and we can look up the exact location for your address.

  • My bin wasn't collected. What do I do?
    ⌄

    Lodge a request via Council's online service request form or call 1300 697 372. Cleanaway will schedule a return collection before your next regular collection day.

    Before you lodge: confirm the bin was at the kerbside by 6:00am and wasn't blocked by a parked vehicle — these are the most common reasons a bin is passed.
  • What day is my bin collected?
    ⌄

    Your collection day is tied to your property address. Search your address using SDRC's online mapping tool to find your bin day, or call 1300 697 372. Collection days may change on public holidays — Council will publish any changes in advance.

  • My bin is damaged or missing. Can I get a replacement?
    ⌄

    Yes. Lodge a request via Council's online service request form or call 1300 697 372. Include your address and describe the issue.

  • I'm elderly or have a disability and can't put my bins out. Is there help available?
    ⌄

    Yes. Council offers an assisted collection service for residents unable to put bins out due to age, disability, or medical immobility. Cleanaway drivers collect and return the bins as part of their normal round. To apply, email mail@sdrc.qld.gov.au with a letter requesting the service and a note or certificate from a registered health practitioner (subject line: ATTN Waste Team).

  • Where do I take items I can't put in the bin?
    ⌄

    For facility locations and hours see the Waste Facility Flyer. For guidance on specific items use Recycle Mate — an interactive tool that finds the nearest disposal option for any item type.

    Asbestos: pre-booking is required. Call 1300 697 372 to arrange before attending.

  • How do I report a pothole or road damage?
    ⌄

    Report online using Council's online service request form — attach a screenshot with a map pin showing the exact location. Alternatively call 1300 697 372. Include the street name, nearest landmark, and an estimate of the size.

  • Is the damaged road Council's or the State Government's responsibility?
    ⌄

    Not all roads in the region are Council's responsibility. Major highways are managed by the Department of Transport and Main Roads (TMR).

    • State roads (New England Hwy, Cunningham Hwy, other TMR roads): contact TMR — 13 20 80
    • Rail crossings / railway tracks: contact Queensland Rail — 13 16 17
    • Council roads: lodge a request with Council as above

    Not sure? Call Council on 1300 697 372 and we can help.

  • How do I check if a road is closed?
    ⌄

    The SDRC Disaster Dashboard has current road closure information and flood camera images of key crossings — the most up-to-date source during weather events. For State roads check Queensland Traffic.

  • My vehicle was damaged by a road defect. Can I make a claim?
    ⌄

    You can submit a letter of demand to Council. Include your name, time and location of the incident, nature of the damage, and any supporting evidence. For road-related claims address to: ATTN: Governance, Southern Downs Regional Council, PO Box 26, Warwick QLD 4370, or email mail@sdrc.qld.gov.au.

  • What disputes can Council help with?
    ⌄

    Council's Local Laws team can investigate: persistent noise nuisances (barking dogs, loud machinery, amplified music); dust, smoke, odour, or light pollution; water pollution from a neighbouring property; unauthorised building works or illegal use of a structure; and unlawful land use.

    Lodge a complaint via Council's online service request form or call 1300 697 372.

  • What disputes are outside Council's jurisdiction?
    ⌄

    Some common disputes are private matters Council cannot mediate or enforce — including boundary and fencing disputes, trees crossing property lines, parking outside the Warwick CBD, and easement disputes. For these matters the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal (QCAT) is often the right avenue: qcat.qld.gov.au or 1300 753 228.

  • There are weeds on a Council road near my property. Who do I contact?
    ⌄

    Council has a dedicated Invasive Pests program with officers responsible for weed control on Council roads and land. Report via Council's online service request form or call 1300 697 372.

    Weeds on the New England Highway or Cunningham Highway? These are State Government roads managed by TMR — contact TMR on 13 20 80.

  • How do I pay my rates?
    ⌄

    Rates can be paid online via SDRC Online Payments, by BPAY (biller code and reference on your notice), or in person at the Warwick or Stanthorpe office. You can also pay by post — cheque payable to Southern Downs Regional Council, PO Box 26, Warwick QLD 4370.

  • I'm struggling to pay my rates. What can I do?
    ⌄

    Contact Council as early as possible on 1300 697 372. Options may include instalment arrangements, hardship provisions, or deferral in exceptional circumstances — the sooner you contact us, the more options are available. See the Payment Difficulties page for more information.

  • How do I receive my rates notice by email instead of post?
    ⌄

    Scan the QR code on page 2 of your rates notice to register for eNotices and receive future notices by email — faster, paperless, and you're notified as soon as your notice is ready.

  • What do the different charges on my rates notice mean?
    ⌄

    The Rates & Charges Explained page breaks down each line item — what it funds and how it's calculated. For a specific question about your notice, call 1300 697 372 and a rates officer can walk through it with you.
